\projectentry
  {Game of Throws Launch Mechanism}
  {Spring-powered mechanical launcher for squash-ball competition}
  {Design \& Construction course project}
  {BSc project, 2024}
  {\portfoliotag{Mechanism design} \portfoliotag{SolidWorks} \portfoliotag{3D printing} \portfoliotag{Laser cutting} \portfoliotag{Testing}}
  {\catapultfigure}
  {This project involved designing and building a spring-powered mechanism for launching a squash ball toward a target plate in the Game of Throws competition. The final design evolved from early catapult and crossbow concepts into an acrylic-and-PLA launcher using a pulley system to increase the ball's acceleration path.

  \begin{projectbullets}
    \item Compared several concepts using a weighted PV evaluation, with focus on fast reloading, sufficient range, adjustability, robustness, and realistic production.
    \item Built and tested multiple prototypes, improving friction, pulley strength, wire routing, locking reliability, and spring utilisation between iterations.
    \item Produced the final mechanism using laser-cut acrylic plates, 3D-printed PLA components, pulleys, wire, a spring, and a mechanical trigger.
    \item Achieved approximately 3 m range and placed 3rd in qualification, while identifying friction and force losses as the main limitations.
  \end{projectbullets}}

\projectentry
  {Audio-Reactive TV Backlight}
  {Analog LED-control circuit driven by a TV audio signal}
  {Electromagnetism capstone project}
  {BSc project, 2026}
  {\portfoliotag{Analog electronics} \portfoliotag{Circuitry} \portfoliotag{Falstad} \portfoliotag{KiCad}}
  {\leddriverfigure}
  {This project involved designing an analog circuit that converts a TV audio signal into brightness control for a standard LED strip. The circuit takes stereo RCA/AUX audio, converts it to mono, removes DC offset, amplifies the signal, smooths it, and uses it to regulate LED current.

  \begin{projectbullets}
    \item Structured the circuit into clear sub-stages: stereo-to-mono input, AC coupling, LM358 amplification, low-pass filtering, and MOSFET-based LED current control.
    \item Added adjustable gain to compensate for different audio-source levels, with the non-inverting amplifier designed for approximately \(3.1\)--\(24.4\) gain.
    \item Used a low-pass filter with an effective cutoff around \(148\,\mathrm{Hz}\) to reduce excessive flicker and produce smoother light response.
    \item Simulated and tuned the design in Falstad before physical testing and presentation.
  \end{projectbullets}}

\projectentry
  {DC Motor Flap Control System}
  {Arduino-controlled position system for an aircraft wing flap model}
  {Control systems project}
  {BSc project}
  {\portfoliotag{DC motor} \portfoliotag{Encoder feedback} \portfoliotag{System identification} \portfoliotag{Bode plots} \portfoliotag{PID control}}
  {\flapfigure}
  {This project models and controls the position of a wing flap actuated by a DC motor. The goal is accurate and predictable angle control, linking the physical electromechanical system to a control-oriented mathematical model.

  \begin{projectbullets}
    \item Modelled the plant with motor dynamics, linkage effects, feedback sensing, and practical limits such as saturation and travel constraints.
    \item Connected measured behaviour with transfer-function analysis in MATLAB, including step-response and frequency-response reasoning.
    \item Integrated Arduino control and encoder feedback to support controller tuning and closed-loop validation.
  \end{projectbullets}}
